The submerged iceberg.

Three marks, all in the locked palette — ink on paper, one ink-blue accent, sharp facets, hard offset shadow, zero gradient. The unifying idea: the waterline is a hairline rule, and the hidden mass below it is a redaction block — the name and the core "encrypted = redacted" motif become the same shape. Tiny tip, huge sealed mass: the proportion is the pitch.

Recommended
Tip hollow, mass solid. The visible peak is an open outline (your "plaintext"); the submerged bulk is a filled ink block (your redaction). Reads cleanly to 16px because it's two shapes split by one rule. Accent variant (right, on ink) puts the ink-blue in the sealed mass. Most on-brand: it *is* the redaction motif.
